The OTHERWORLDLY AURORA borealis, or northern lights, beging high the Earth's atmosphere-at altitudes from 60 to more than 250 miles- when charged particles from the sun become trapped in the earth's magnetic field. The result is a colorful, dancing light show.

The first I filmed an aurora was from an icebreacker near antarctica. Luckily, there are more accessible places if you'd like to take in the shows yourself.
